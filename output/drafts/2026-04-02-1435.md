Spent the last 3 months building something I wish existed when I was drowning in hour-long YouTube videos.

Meet InsightClips — an AI tool that turns lengthy YouTube content into short, digestible clips with the key insights extracted automatically.

Here's why I built it (and what I learned):

**The problem was personal**
I kept bookmarking 2-hour podcasts and never getting back to them. Or I'd watch something great, forget the key points, and couldn't find that one insight I wanted to reference later.

Sound familiar?

**What InsightClips actually does:**
1. Takes any YouTube URL (up to 2 hours now — just bumped it from 90 minutes)
2. Uses AI to identify the most valuable moments
3. Creates short clips with context-aware summaries
4. Lets you save and organize insights for later

**The technical reality check:**
I originally built this with polling-based transcription. Worked fine for testing, but as soon as I had multiple users, everything slowed to a crawl.

Spent hours debugging what turned out to be a classic async problem.

Solution? Switched to webhook-based transcription with AssemblyAI. Night and day difference.

**231 commits later**, here's what surprised me:
- The hardest part wasn't the AI — it was handling video processing at scale
- Users wanted longer videos supported (hence the 120-minute limit)
- People don't just want clips, they want a personal knowledge base

This connects to something I noticed while building VibeWatch and Word Chaos — the real value isn't just in the AI output, it's in how you organize and surface that output later.

InsightClips isn't just a video summarizer. It's becoming a personal learning archive.

What would you use this for? Curious to know what types of long-form content you struggle to get through.
